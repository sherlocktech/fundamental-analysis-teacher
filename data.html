The higher the ratio, the greater the likelihood the company will be able to pay its bills. The value of this ratio should be greater than 1; many analysts consider values under 2 reason for concern. The higher the ratio the better (though the quick ratio will always be lower than the current ratio). Typical values vary by industry, ranging from about 1 in industries whose production is capital intensive (e.g., steel and autos) to over 10 in some types of retailing. TA higher ratio suggests that, on average, its goods are moving quickly; a lower ratio suggests that the firm may be saddled with obsolete inventory or is having trouble selling its product.
